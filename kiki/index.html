set up your hub

the hub is optional — kiki works on its own as an encrypted vault. add it when you want to sync files across devices or keep an encrypted backup on your Mac or NAS.

1

open Terminal on your Mac

search for Terminal in Spotlight (⌘ Space, type “Terminal”).

2

run the installer

paste this and press Return. it downloads the hub, installs it, and sets it to start automatically on login.

bash <(curl -fsSL https://arcanedata.us/kiki/hub/install.sh)
3

open kiki on your iPhone

make sure your iPhone is on the same Wi-Fi as your Mac. kiki finds the hub automatically — you should see “connected” within a few seconds.

4

import your first file

tap “import file” on the home screen. kiki encrypts it and syncs it to your hub.

1

SSH into your NAS

enable SSH in your NAS admin panel (Synology: Control Panel → Terminal & SNMP → Enable SSH). then connect from your Mac: ssh admin@your-nas-ip

2

run the NAS installer

paste this into the SSH session and press Return. it detects your architecture, downloads the right binary, and sets it to start automatically.

bash <(curl -fsSL https://arcanedata.us/kiki/hub/nas-install.sh)

works on Synology DSM 7+, QNAP, Unraid, TrueNAS Scale, and any Linux NAS.

3

connect kiki to your NAS

the installer prints your NAS’s IP address at the end. open kiki on your iPhone → Settings → hub url and enter it:

http://your-nas-ip:8787

if your NAS is on the same network as your iPhone, kiki may also find it automatically — no manual entry needed.

4

import your first file

tap “import file” on the home screen. kiki encrypts it and syncs it to your NAS.

can't find the hub?

if your iPhone shows "no hub discovered", check that both devices are on the same Wi-Fi network (not a guest network). if discovery still doesn't work, open Settings in kiki and enter the hub address manually — the installer prints it at the end.
